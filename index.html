Who am I?

Currently finishing my PhD thesis at the University of Zurich, I am busy working on searches for heavy resonances decaying to dibosons with the CMS detector as well as a deep neural network for W-tagging. I work for one of the two general purpose experiments at CERN, CMS, where I specialize in searches for exotic resonances as well as jets and jet substructure.

Based at the University of Zurich, where I teach, I frequently visit CERN in Geneva where I have my second office. I am a huge enthusiast of deep learning and have spent the past year working on DNNs of my own as well as supervising two students on Deep Learning projects.

As any particle physicist at CERN, I spend most of my days being a combination of a data scientist, statistician, experimental physicist, teacher and software engineer. CERN is a vibrating and inspiring community, where I daily work together with people from all around the globe who are experts in their field. At the University of Zurich I get to do outreach and teach bright Bachelor and Master students in Physics, something I find equally inspiring!
